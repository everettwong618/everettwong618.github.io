What is MMET?

Manufacturing and Mechanical Engineering Technology is about how products and physical systems get designed, built, tested, documented, and improved in real environments. It combines CAD, materials, mechanics, data, and clear technical communication.

In plain English: I learn how physical systems work, how to document them clearly, and how to make decisions that hold up under real constraints. That same systems mindset carries into the way I build websites.

Selected work

The problem, the decisions, and what shipped.

Featured project · Event invitation tool

Skhedule

A web app that helps event hosts fill limited-capacity events without overbooking by sending invitations in controlled stages.

  • For Hosts with a ranked guest list longer than available seats
  • Role Sole designer and builder
  • Live skhedule.vercel.app

Problem

Inviting everyone at once risks overbooking. Inviting one person at a time is slow. Hosts need a system that invites just enough people, waits for RSVPs or timeouts, then moves down the list.

What I built

  • Ranked guest CSV upload and staged invitation rounds
  • Email invites with Yes/No RSVP links (guests do not need an account)
  • Host dashboard: Acceptances, Outstanding, and Not yet invited
  • Automatic timeouts, scheduled processing, Postgres hosting, and tests

Why it mattered

The hard part is staying correct while people reply at different times — never over-invite, treat timeouts as declines, and only start the next round when seats open.

Result

Shipped a working MVP at skhedule.vercel.app with a full invite → RSVP → seats-filled flow.
